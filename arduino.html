My Partner Kyle and I worked on making a Red-Light Green-Light Game setup using the Arduino Board. To achieve this, we used four RGB LED's, 1 IR Remote, and many jumper wires. We faced many difficulties while making the baord, most of which carried relatively simple solutions. To start, we were going to begin with Red and Green LED's, but this would mean we could only use two of each. That is, until we found out we could use RGB LED's, summing up to four LED's that would be the same colors all at the same time. Our biggest problem did not arrive until we began to impliment the IR Remote. Immediately after connecting the Reciever to the circuit, it did not want to work. In fact, we began to smell something funky, and realised we hooked up the 5V and recieving pins. We fixed that but still nothing was showing that the reciever was working. And so we decided to unplug the whole board, and redo the wiring. We still met the same problem. It was until another day passed that we realized we left the IR remote untouched the entire and by clicking the buttons would show the IR reciever DOES work. Great. An entire three days wasted because we could not read instructions. But I digress. Now, knowing the Reciever works, we finally worked on the code, or should I say myself, as Kyle wanted the rewire the board just so it would look more pleasing, and having done three times now, was pretty easy for him to do. Coding for me was quite easy, I had to write a code that would change the color of the LED depending on what color is pressed. If Button 1 is pressed, the LEDs would change Red. If Button 2 is pressed, the LEDs will change Green.
